Feeding

  • Misc
    • Wait 20-30 minutes to feed after a big running outside

    • Feed around 7-8 in the AM and PM

    • Give yourself time to be present for the feedings in the morning

  • Food Storage
    • Located in hallway closet
    • White pastic bin on bottom left
    • Metal sccoper inside bowl

  • Serving Size
    • Bruce: 1 heaping scoop in green plastic bowl
    • Penny: 1 heaping scoop in silver metal bowl

  • The Ritual

    • Penny:
      • Give her the BED command and lead her to the green dog bed

      • Place her bowl on the bed

      • If she doesn't eat after about 10 minutes:
        • Add a littl cold water, mix and sit for a minute

        • Still no dice? Add some peanut butter

        • Nothing? Place in the fridge until next feeding

        • Note: It's ok if Penny skips a single meal. If she's skipping two or more - give us a shout. If we can't be reached, schedule a vet visit (contact page)

    • Bruce:
      • Give him the CRATE command and lead him to the crate

      • Place the bowl in the crate

      • Remove bowl as soon as he's done eating

      • Keep him in crate until Penny has finished eating

    Treats
    • Treat Storage
      • Everyday treats in glass jar on kitchen dolly
        • 2-3 per day

      • Assortment of treats in basket in cabinet above stove:
        • Dental Treat: 1 per day
        • Rawhide chews: 1 per day

    Walks and Off-Leash Runs

    • Misc.
      • Dog's should be walked at MINIMUM 3x per day
        • Morning
        • Back from work
        • Evening

      • If you're home with the pups for the day

      • One of the daily walks should be an off-leash romping at the park

      • Walks should be 20-30 minutes minimum

      • Off-leash runs can be 45 min to 1 hr if not too hot

      • MUST bring water and collapsable bowl on hot days for off-leash rompings

    • Equipment Check
      • Collars
      • Leashes
      • Poop Bags
      • Apartment Keys
      • Tennis ball launcher (for off-leash time)
      • Water bottle and collapsable bowl (for off-leash time)

    • Basic Walks
      • 20-30 minute walks around the neighborhood

      • Best is to head south down Van Brunt and explore side streets on the east side

      • Walking around the block and through the park is also a nice path

        • Bruce:
          • Bruce will likely go to the bathroom immediately

          • He responds well to HEAL command while walking


        • Penny:
          • Penny will likley NOT go to the bathroom right away and will need more walking around time

          • Penny may go the entire walk and not go to the bathroom, but she'll usually at least pee

          • Penny will almost always go #1 and #2 at the park - even if on the leash. This is your go to if you're having issues

    • Off-Leash Runs:
      • Coffee Park Is an off-leash during specic hours of the day and is one block from the apartment. Go and go often!
        • 6:00AM - 9:00AM
        • 9:00PM - 1:00AM

      • The larger areas of grass on the left side of the park are ideal

      • Keep an eye on them at all times and don't hesitate to call them (assertively) to you if they are:
        • Wandering too close near the exits of the park
        • Chasing after other dogs (or wanting to say hi to humans) all the way across the park

      • Penny and Bruce love to chase eachother. Don't be alarmed at their barking and nipping. As long as their tails are up and waging, all is good

      • Penny loves to play fetch with the tennis launcher. Bruce not so much

      • Bring water/bowl and encourage drink breaks

      • Use COME command and call names to bring them in

      • Valention Jr. Pier Park. is another great option. Located south west of the apartment, it's about a 10-15 minute walk and away from any main roads (and near the water). Highly recommend going to this park on a weekend morning.

    • Dog Park Watchouts
      • Bruce and Penny are very friendly, but not every other dog is. If you ever feel like other dogs or Penny and Bruce are being too aggressive durring play, calmly walk over, clap loudly over the dogs, leash up and leave.

      • Coffee Park is nice, but the grass is often let to grow longer than it should and BBQs on the weekend leave hidden treasures. Keep an eye out for the dogs sniffing and eating discarded food

    Leaving the Pups for the Day Checklist

    • Make sure you have the keys!
    • Put Bruce in his create - latch it
    • Penny is fine on her own in the apt
    • Be sure water bowl is full
    • Make sure AC is on, but you can raise the temp to 73
    Bedtime
    • Bruce sleeps in the create
    • Penny can sleep on her green bed. She prefers sleeping in the actual bedroom bed, but that's your preference (she's a great cuddler)
    • Feel free to close the bedroom door. You may hear pacing outside the door. It will eventually stop.
    Commands

    • Be assertive when giving all commands. Both dogs respond well to an authoritative voice

    • Both respond to their names, but you may need to be repetitive and loud with all commands if distracted by people you encounter walking or dogs in the park

    • Basic Commands:
      • HEEL command when walking. They should both be on the left
      • COME command
      • SIT command
      • NO or PSSST command to stop them doing something unwanted
      • DROP IT command if eating something in the park or off the street in the park
      • BED command for Penny
      • CRATE command for Bruce
    Special Bruce Instructions

    • He’s still a puppy and will likely test your patience :)

    • He sometimes whines in the early morning. If you're not ready to get up, just go out and give him a big no and put a blanket over his crate

    • He’s fine to wait to be taken out until 7:30-8:00 if need be

    • It’s best to keep him from drinking water past 8PM

    • If he’s being a pain and can’t settle down, just put him in his crate, give him a treat and close the door. He may whine or bark. Ignore and do not make eye contact. If he continues for a minute or more, give him a big NO and put a blanket over the crate. He will eventually calm down

    • He eats grass and sticks like it’s a job. It’s not a big deal, but do try and keep him from doing so by distracting him as best you can

    Baths (if needed)

    • If they get really muddy or sandy, or got into something nasty at the park, or if you just want to give them a bath:
      • Shampoo is located in the bottom long draw under the bathroom sink. Use any of the options

      • Use the white loofa in the shower

      • Flip the small lever behind the actual shower head up top to flow the water to the hanging shower head

      • Make sure the water is luke-warm - doesn’t need to be hot or even warm. They’re sensitive to the temps

      • There are two light-green (sea foam) towels in the hallway closet, towards the bottom left

      • Wet down the dog, wash with a soapy loofa and rinse

      • Dry them off as best you can and then put the towels down on the crate and beds and have them chill out a bit until they’re dry

      • Blankets on top of them will be appreciated
